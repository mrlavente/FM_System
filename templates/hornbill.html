• Profile: Visayan Writhed Hornbill

Species Profile

Species Name:Visayan Writhed Hornbill

Scientific Name: Aceros waldeni

Common Name: Dulungan, Kalaw

Habitat:Low-elevation to midmountain transition forest

Conservation Status:Critically Endangered

Threats: Loss of Habitat and Hunting

Description

Walden's hornbill (Aceros waldeni) locally called dulungan, also known as the Visayan wrinkled hornbill, rufous-headed hornbill or writhed-billed hornbill, is a critically endangered species of hornbill living in the rainforests on the islands of Negros and Panay in the Philippines. It is closely related to the writhed hornbill, but can be recognized by the yellow throat and ocular skin in the male, and the blue throat and ocular skin in the female (both throat and ocular skin are deep orange or red in both sexes of the writhed hornbill). Its binomial name commemorates the Scottish ornithologist Viscount Walden.

Behavior: The Visayan Writhed Hornbill mates for life or monogamous. During the mating season the male will build a nest in order to attract a potential mate.

Mating Season: September-December

Seasonality of Disease: Due to nutritional imbalance, they may suffer from Iron storage diseases especially during mating season wherein they needed a lot of nutients.
