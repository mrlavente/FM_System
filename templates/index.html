Focus of the System

In ecology, an endemic species is one that is unique to the area in which it is found. A species may be native to a specific geographic region, such as a single island, or a wider geographical area, such as a continent. Plants and animals that are endemic to a specific geographic area are known as endemic species. Some species are endemic to a specific continent, while others are endemic to a specific portion of a continent, and still others are endemic to a single island.

Time-Series Based Population Forecasting

The Time Series Based-Population Forecasting and Monitoring System have the capacity to provide a Monitoring system that solely focus on the conservation of the endemic species of Panay Island to the DENR and respective parks through the analyzed data. This proposal can help the authorities to monitor the endemic species and the conservation efforts more efficiently.
